# Getting started: Convicta News

Convicta News is our general product and is open to everyone. Here's how to go from zero to a personalized feed in a couple of minutes.

## 1. Start the bot

Open **[@Convicta_news_bot](https://t.me/Convicta_news_bot)** in Telegram and tap **Start** (or send `/start`).

The bot greets you and offers **"Set up your feed"**, which opens the Convicta News **Mini App** — the hub where everything is configured.

## 2. Get your feed instantly

You don't have to configure anything to start seeing value. New users are automatically given a **World-wires preview** — a curated stream of world news — so your feed is never empty on day one.

## 3. Set your language

Convicta News is multilingual. Pick your language in the Mini App, and the interface follows your choice.

> **Warming up during community testing:** automatic *translation* of headlines into your language is an AI-assisted feature that is metered and may be limited while we test. When it's paused, foreign-language stories may appear in their original language (typically English) rather than translated. The feed still works — the translation layer is what's warming up.

## 4. Choose your categories

Open the Mini App hub and select the **categories** you care about. Convicta filters, de-duplicates, and tags incoming stories, so you get each story once, sorted into the topics you picked — instead of a wall of near-identical reposts.

## 5. Your first digest

From the hub you can enable a **daily digest** — a once-a-day roundup instead of a running stream. You can adjust digest settings, presets, and configuration from the same hub.

> **Note:** the digest and the advanced source/OPML setup wizard are part of the paid **News Standard** plan. During the free-2-months trial you have access to try them. See [Pricing]({{ '/pricing/' | relative_url }}).

## What you get for free vs. paid

- **Free:** the World-wires preview feed, category selection, deduplication and tagging, and the "why you got this" context on each card.
- **News Standard (paid, free during the 2-month trial):** add your own sources, import an **OPML** file, and configure the digest wizard.

## A note on timing

News matching runs on a regular **batch cadence** — Convicta is designed to be *timely and complete*, not millisecond-by-millisecond real-time. Treat it as a feed that keeps you current, not a live ticker.
