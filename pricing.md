# Pricing & the free-2-months trial

## The headline: free for 2 months

While Convicta is a community testing version, **both products are free for your first two months.** New Convicta News users get a 60-day trial, and new Polymarket Pulse users get a 60-day grant on the full-featured tier. Access is free right now because we're still collecting feedback and some features aren't yet working exactly as intended — you don't need to pay to try the complete experience while we test.

After your two months, you can stay on a free tier or subscribe to a paid plan. Nothing charges automatically without you choosing a plan.

All paid plans can be billed **monthly or annually — annual = 2 months free** (you pay for 10 months, get 12). Payment is via **CryptoPay** ([@CryptoBot](https://t.me/CryptoBot)), charged as the USD-equivalent in crypto.

Pulse is **a community testing version**. Plans are paid through **CryptoPay** (via [@CryptoBot](https://t.me/CryptoBot)).

- **Pulse Pro** is **$12/month** or **$120/year**; **Pulse Edge** is **$20/month** or **$200/year** (two months free on annual).
- **Pulse Edge includes News+** — there is no separate bundle to buy.
- Every new Pulse user starts with a **two-month free grant** on the full-featured tier during the testing phase.

> **About the Edge read:** Edge is an AI-computed fair-value estimate that improves over time as our models and data get better. It's an additional metric that may help you make a decision on prediction markets — it is **not financial advice**, and the decision is yours. See [Features]({{ '/features/' | relative_url }}).

> **A reminder while we're testing:** the AI-assisted surfaces (summaries, translations, and the Pulse **Edge** read) are metered and may be limited or paused while Convicta is in community testing — see [Features]({{ '/features/' | relative_url }}). Your plan's rule-based features (feed, dedup, price/resolution/trader alerts) run normally regardless.

- **Pulse** payments go through CryptoPay (@CryptoBot).
- Web sign-in (Google / email / Telegram) ties your subscription to your account, so it carries across Telegram and the web.
